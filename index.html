What is a Tasmanian Devil?

Sarcophilus Harrisii

A heavily built marsupial with a large head, powerful jaws, and mainly black fur, found only in Tasmania. It is slow-moving and aggressive, feeding mainly on carrion.

More About the Tasmanian Devil

Population

The Tasmanian devil are only found on the island, state of Tasmania, a part of Australia. In 1996 at the peak of their population, there were an estimated 250,000 living. Now, as little as 10% of the Tasmanian devil population remains.

Role in Ecosystems

Tasmanian devils play a crucial role in keeping the local ecosystem in balance. If they go extinct, the foxes and feral cats population could explode, and dozens of mammals species, many which are unique to Tasmania, would be wiped out. In the end, all Tasmanian wildlife could suffer if the Tasmanian devils will cease to exist.

Devil Facial Tumour Disease(DFTD)

The DFTD is a parasitic cancer first introduced in 1996. Ways of transferring the disease include devil biting devil, consuming infected carcass or sharing food. Some symptoms include lumps or sores around the lips and mouth of the devil. Organism dies within six months due to organ failure, secondary infection, or starvation caused by the inability to feed.

What is being done to save the Tasmanian devil?

The 'Save the Tasmanian Devil' program was established to investigate the disease and condition of wild devil populations through extensive monitoring. There have been some developments in treating and preventing the disease, which does give hope to remaining devils.
